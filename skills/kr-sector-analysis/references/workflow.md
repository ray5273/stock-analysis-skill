# Workflow Reference

## Source Order

Use this order unless the user explicitly asks for a faster but lighter answer:

1. Ministries, regulators, KOSIS, Statistics Korea, Bank of Korea, public agencies
2. Industry institutes, associations, support-program notices, policy briefings
3. KRX filings, DART filings, and listed-company IR materials
4. Reputable trade publications or business media for context

## Mode Routing

- `quick brief`
  Use for compressed decision support, one-page strategy notes, or when the source set is still forming.
- `full report`
  Use for broader industry requests covering market structure, policy, competition, and listed-company mapping.

If the request is ambiguous and the scope is still loose, run the `kr-sector-plan` flow first.

## Core Checklist

### Sector Definition

- Define the sector clearly.
- State what is included and excluded.
- Explain whether the sector is demand-led, infrastructure-led, or service-led.

### Market Landscape

- Summarize the current market structure.
- Separate what is directly observed from what is inferred.
- State whether the market is fragmented, concentrated, policy-driven, or capital-intensive.

### Drivers and Constraints

- Identify 3-5 durable drivers.
- Identify 3-5 real constraints.
- Use dated policy or company evidence where possible.

### Value Chain

- Map upstream infrastructure and equipment, midstream operators or integrators, and downstream customers.
- Distinguish listed and private participants.

### Regulation and Technology

- Capture regulations, reporting burdens, permitting bottlenecks, energy constraints, and announced reforms.
- Capture technology shifts only when they clearly matter for economics, utilization, or competitive position.

### Public-Company Map

- Use public companies only when you can explain the exposure path.
- Label companies as direct, partial, or indirect exposure.
- Say when listed pure-play exposure is thin.

### Conclusion

- State where the near-term opportunity likely sits.
- State what can go wrong.
- State what would change the view.

## Failure Modes To Avoid

- importing global market numbers without saying they are not Korea-only
- turning company marketing pages into market-size evidence
- pretending every sector has clean public pure-plays
- flattening mixed-date statistics into one synthetic snapshot
