# Workflow Reference

## Goal

Produce a source-dated input pack that another agent can turn into a sector brief or report.

## Source Order

Use this order unless the user explicitly asks for a faster but lighter answer:

1. Ministries, regulators, KOSIS, Bank of Korea, Statistics Korea, public agencies
2. Industry institutes, associations, and official support-program notices
3. KRX filings, DART filings, and listed-company IR materials
4. Reputable trade publications and business media for context only

## Minimum Data Pack Fields

- sector definition
- exact source window
- Korea-only metrics and dates when available
- policy or regulatory events with dates
- value-chain map
- representative listed companies and why they fit
- known blind spots or undisclosed fields

## Collection Checklist

### Market Definition

- Define the sector in plain language.
- State where adjacent sectors begin and end.
- Note whether the sector is supply-side, demand-side, or full-stack.

### Metrics

- Gather market size, growth, installed base, utilization, order backlog, or adoption metrics only when source-backed.
- Record exact dates and units.
- If no clean Korea-only number exists, say so directly.

### Policy and Regulation

- Capture funding programs, licensing rules, reporting obligations, power or environmental constraints, and announced reforms.
- Keep dates exact.

### Company and Value Chain

- Map representative listed companies to their role.
- Distinguish pure-play, partial exposure, and indirect beneficiaries.
- Note where private players dominate and listed exposure is thin.

## Failure Modes To Avoid

- mixing interpretation into a supposedly structured pack
- citing undated marketing pages as if they were fresh market metrics
- hiding missing data
- using company marketing language as sector evidence without labeling it
