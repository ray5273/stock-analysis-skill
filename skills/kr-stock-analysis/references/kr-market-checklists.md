# Korea Market-Specific Checklists

Use these lists after completing the base workflow. Keep the analysis tied to current DART filings, KRX disclosures, ETF documents, and market data.

## KRX Growth Stock Checklist

### Business and Demand Quality

- Check whether growth is driven by volume, pricing, mix, exports, or one-off cycle strength.
- Check whether growth depends on one customer, one memory cycle, one product ramp, or one policy tailwind.
- Check whether backlog, order book, utilization, or ASP trend supports the growth narrative.
- Check whether new capacity or aggressive capex could pressure returns.
- Check whether management still has a credible long runway or whether saturation is appearing.

### Unit Economics and Reinvestment

- Check gross margin or operating margin trend and whether scale is improving incremental returns.
- Check capex intensity and whether free cash flow conversion trails accounting earnings.
- Check R&D intensity and whether it is creating durable product advantage.
- Check working capital swings, especially inventory and receivables.
- Check whether growth is bought through low-return expansion.

### Financial Quality

- Check whether growth is accelerating or decelerating on a comparable basis.
- Check whether export mix, FX, or commodity inputs are driving reported results.
- Check whether management guidance or IR commentary implies a credible margin path.
- Check net cash versus net debt, refinancing dependence, and funding flexibility.
- Check whether acquisitions or subsidiaries are masking underlying organic slowdown.

### Valuation and Expectations

- Prefer P/E, EV/EBITDA, EV/EBIT, FCF yield, or price to sales depending on the business model.
- Compare valuation against growth durability and margin potential, not growth alone.
- Check whether the stock already prices in a full cycle recovery or perfect execution.
- Stress test the thesis for both earnings misses and multiple compression.

### Key Risks

- Cycle peak mistaken for structural growth
- Margin disappointment after heavy capex
- FX or export demand reversal
- Subsidy or policy normalization
- One-quarter guide-down breaking the narrative

## KRX Value Stock Checklist

### Cheap or Just Impaired

- Identify why the stock looks cheap: cyclicality, governance discount, holding-company discount, asset fear, weak returns on capital, or structural decline.
- Check whether earnings are above or below a normalized cycle level.
- Check whether consensus is extrapolating peak margins or trough margins.
- Check whether book value reflects realizable asset value after hidden losses or stale carrying values.
- Check whether reported cheapness is driven by one-time accounting items.

### Balance Sheet and Asset Quality

- Check leverage against normalized earnings, not only trailing results.
- Check debt maturities, floating-rate exposure, covenant risk, lease liabilities, and guarantees.
- Check working capital intensity and whether cash conversion deteriorates in downturns.
- Check inventory quality, reserve adequacy, or credit quality when relevant.
- Check off-balance-sheet obligations and contingent liabilities.

### Capital Allocation and Governance

- Check whether buybacks are cancelled or simply held as treasury shares indefinitely.
- Check dividend coverage and payout stability through the cycle.
- Check whether management has a history of value-destructive acquisitions or unfair related-party moves.
- Check insider incentives and whether controlling shareholder incentives diverge from minorities.
- Check whether restructuring, spin-offs, treasury share cancellation, or policy shifts have a credible path to unlock value.

### Valuation

- Prefer normalized P/E, EV/EBIT, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield, P/B, dividend yield, or sum-of-the-parts depending on the business.
- Bridge from current earnings to mid-cycle earnings.
- Compare the stock to peers on both quality and valuation to avoid false bargains.
- Check what rerating is plausible without assuming heroic margin recovery.
- Separate asset value support from earnings power support.

### Value Trap Risks

- Structural demand decline mistaken for cyclical weakness
- Governance discount that does not close
- Weak capital allocation nullifying cheap valuation
- Cheap multiple justified by low returns on capital
- Thesis depending entirely on rerating with no operating improvement

## Holding Company Checklist

- Identify listed stakes, unlisted stakes, net cash, debt, and treasury shares.
- Estimate look-through NAV and note key assumptions.
- Check whether major subsidiaries trade at high or low valuations versus history.
- Check whether the discount has a catalyst to close or is structurally persistent.
- Check governance events, succession risk, and restructuring probability.

## Korean ETF Checklist

### Fund Construction

- Identify the exact fund, sponsor, benchmark index, and replication method.
- Check whether the ETF is physical, synthetic, levered, inverse, actively managed, or currency hedged.
- Check rebalance rules and whether they create turnover or crowding risk.
- Check number of holdings, top-10 concentration, and single-name exposure.
- Check sector, theme, market-cap, and style tilt versus what the user thinks they are buying.

### Cost and Execution

- Check total expense ratio, embedded costs, and any synthetic swap structure.
- Check average trading value, bid-ask spread, and assets under management.
- Check liquidity of the underlying basket, not just headline ETF volume.
- Check tracking error and NAV gap history when available.
- Check roll cost, swap cost, or derivatives drag if applicable.

### Portfolio and Exposure Quality

- Check overlap with common alternatives when relevant.
- Check whether top holdings dominate the outcome.
- Check valuation of the underlying basket if the user is effectively buying expensive exposure through an ETF wrapper.
- Check theme drift or methodology drift over time.
- Check whether derivatives or offshore exposure introduce hidden basis risk.

### ETF Risks

- Concentration risk hidden behind broad branding
- Low AUM and closure risk
- Wide spreads despite low headline fee
- Methodology producing unintended factor bets
- NAV gap or tracking issues during volatile markets
