# U.S. Style-Specific Checklists

Use these lists after completing the base workflow. Keep the analysis tied to current filings, fund documents, and market data.

## U.S. Growth Stock Checklist

### Business and Demand Quality

- Check whether growth is driven by volume, pricing, mix, acquisitions, or temporary pull-forward.
- Check whether revenue is recurring, usage-based, transactional, or hardware-led.
- Check customer concentration, churn risk, and net revenue retention when available.
- Check whether growth depends on one product cycle, one platform partner, or one narrow end market.
- Check whether management still has a credible total addressable market argument or whether saturation is appearing.

### Unit Economics and Reinvestment

- Check gross margin trend and whether scale is improving incremental margins.
- Check sales and marketing efficiency, payback logic, and whether growth is becoming more expensive to buy.
- Check research and development intensity and whether it is producing durable product advantage.
- Check free cash flow conversion relative to reported earnings.
- Check stock-based compensation as a percent of revenue and as a share count outcome.

### Financial Quality

- Check whether growth is accelerating or decelerating on a comparable basis.
- Check deferred revenue, remaining performance obligations, bookings, or backlog if they matter.
- Check whether management is guiding to a credible margin expansion path.
- Check cash runway, net cash versus net debt, and refinancing dependence.
- Check whether acquisitions are masking organic slowdown.

### Valuation and Expectations

- Prefer EV/sales, EV/gross profit, forward free cash flow, or PEG-style framing when appropriate.
- Compare valuation against growth durability and margin potential, not growth alone.
- Check whether the stock already prices in a near-perfect execution path.
- Check the earnings multiple on mature-margin assumptions, not just current losses.
- Stress test the thesis for multiple compression even if the company keeps growing.

### Key Risks

- Narrative break from slowing growth
- Margin disappointment after heavy hiring or capex
- SBC dilution outrunning intrinsic value growth
- Platform, regulatory, or AI displacement risk
- One-quarter guide-down triggering multiple compression

## U.S. Value Stock Checklist

### Cheap or Just Impaired

- Identify why the stock looks cheap: cyclicality, regulatory pressure, asset fear, litigation, governance discount, or structural decline.
- Check whether earnings are above or below a normalized cycle level.
- Check whether consensus is extrapolating peak margins or trough margins.
- Check whether book value or tangible book actually reflects realizable asset value.
- Check whether reported cheapness is driven by one-time accounting gains or losses.

### Balance Sheet and Asset Quality

- Check leverage against normalized earnings, not only trailing results.
- Check debt maturities, floating-rate exposure, covenant risk, and pension or lease liabilities.
- Check working capital intensity and whether cash conversion deteriorates in downturns.
- Check inventory quality, reserve adequacy, or credit quality when relevant.
- Check off-balance-sheet obligations and contingent liabilities.

### Capital Allocation

- Check whether buybacks occurred at sensible valuations or simply offset dilution.
- Check dividend coverage through the cycle, not just in the last year.
- Check whether management has a history of value-destructive acquisitions.
- Check insider incentives and whether management is paid for per-share value creation.
- Check whether asset sales, spin-offs, or restructuring have a credible path to unlock value.

### Valuation

- Prefer normalized P/E, EV/EBIT, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield, price to tangible book, or sum-of-the-parts depending on the business.
- Bridge from current earnings to mid-cycle earnings.
- Compare the stock to peers on both quality and valuation to avoid false bargains.
- Check what rerating is plausible without assuming heroic margin recovery.
- Separate asset value support from earnings power support.

### Value Trap Risks

- Structural demand decline mistaken for cyclical weakness
- Hidden liabilities or weak asset quality
- Management destroying value with poor capital allocation
- Cheap multiple justified by low returns on capital
- Thesis depending entirely on multiple expansion with no operating improvement

## ETF Checklist

### Fund Construction

- Identify the exact fund, sponsor, benchmark index, and replication method.
- Check whether the ETF is cap-weighted, equal-weighted, factor-based, actively managed, levered, or inverse.
- Check reconstitution and rebalance rules and whether they create turnover or momentum-chasing risk.
- Check number of holdings, top-10 concentration, and single-name exposure.
- Check sector, geography, market-cap, and style tilts versus what the user thinks they are buying.

### Cost and Execution

- Check expense ratio, fee waivers, and whether the waiver has an expiration date.
- Check average daily volume, bid-ask spread, and assets under management.
- Check creation-redemption health and whether liquidity mostly comes from the underlying basket.
- Check tracking difference versus the benchmark, not just stated expense ratio.
- Check whether securities lending revenue offsets part of the fee or introduces extra risk.

### Portfolio and Exposure Quality

- Check overlap with common alternatives such as SPY, VOO, IVV, QQQ, VTI, or sector ETFs when relevant.
- Check whether the top holdings dominate the outcome.
- Check valuation of the underlying basket if the user is effectively buying expensive growth through an ETF wrapper.
- Check style drift over time, especially for thematic or rules-based funds.
- Check derivatives use, sampling, futures, swaps, or currency hedging if applicable.

### Tax and Distribution Considerations

- Check distribution yield, distribution quality, and whether income comes from dividends, options, or capital gains.
- Check whether the structure is an ETF, ETN, CEF, grantor trust, or commodity pool.
- Check tax treatment quirks if the fund holds futures, MLPs, or non-U.S. assets.

### ETF Risks

- Concentration risk hidden behind broad branding
- Low AUM and closure risk
- Wide spreads despite low expense ratio
- Index methodology producing unintended factor bets
- Tracking issues during volatile markets
